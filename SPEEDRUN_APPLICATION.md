### What problem are you solving?

Productivity apps assume you're already in a functional mental state. They don't.
85% of knowledge workers report regular overwhelm. When someone is anxious or mentally flooded, opening Notion or Todoist makes it worse — the blank page, the backlog, the structured fields.

The result: people avoid their tools exactly when they need them most. Thoughts stay stuck in their heads. Tasks get dropped. The mental load compounds.

There is no app that meets people where they are emotionally *and* gets things done.

---

### What is Synq and how does it work?

Synq is a judgment-free capture layer that sits between your brain and your productivity system.

**Voice-first AI capture:** You say *"Ugh, I'm feeling overwhelmed. Need to buy groceries, call Mom — call her today at 5pm — and finish the report."* Synq's NLP parser:
- Detects the emotion → logs it to your mood tracker (sad, intensity 4/5)
- Extracts tasks → adds them to your thought list (buy groceries, finish report)
- Detects the time reference → creates a reminder: "Call Mom · Today 17:00"

All in one sentence. Zero form-filling.

**Everything else it does:**
- Emotion log with 7-day mood chart (pattern recognition)
- Box breathing exercise (4-4-4-4) for overwhelm moments
- Smart reminders with calendar view
- Swipe-to-complete thoughts with undo
- Dark/light mode, pull-to-refresh
- AI companion chat ("Talk to Synq")
- Full offline-first PWA

### Who are your target customers?

**Primary:** Adults 22–38 with ADHD, anxiety, or high cognitive load (freelancers, founders, caregivers, students). These users fail with standard productivity tools because the tools assume executive function they often don't have.

**Secondary:** Anyone who has ever said "I have too much going on" — which is ~half the working population.

**Wedge market:** Mental health-adjacent productivity (ADHD tools, therapy journaling). Estimated 45M people in the US alone report ADHD symptoms. Tools like Goblin Tools ($2M ARR, no investors) prove there's strong demand and willingness to pay.

### Why now?

Three things converged in the last 18 months:
1. **LLMs made real-time NLP cheap** — parsing a voice transcript into structured tasks+reminders+emotions costs <$0.001 per capture in 2026
2. **Voice input crossed mainstream** — 67% of US adults now use voice input weekly (vs. 31% in 2021)
3. **Mental health destigmatization** — people will pay for tools that acknowledge their emotional state; Headspace at $2B valuation proved that

We're building the convergence product: voice-first, emotionally aware, AI-organized.

### What is your unfair advantage / moat?

1. **Emotional framing as product philosophy, not feature** — Every design decision in Synq starts with "how does this make the user feel?" This is hard to copy because it requires fundamentally rethinking UX, not just adding an emotion toggle.

2. **Voice NLP parser trained on "overwhelmed speech"** — Our parser handles fragmented, emotional, run-on speech ("I feel bad but I need to... and also...") that standard task parsers fail on. Built with a custom grammar model, not just GPT prompting.

3. **Distribution through mental health communities** — r/ADHD (1.7M members), therapist partner channels, and ADHD coaches are early acquisition vectors that don't require paid ads. Trust-based virality in communities that are actively seeking tools like this.
